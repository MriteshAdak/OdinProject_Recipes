Dal Fry


Dal Fry is a delicious and popular Indian lentil recipe that is made with arhar dal (tuvar or toor dal in Hindi and pigeon pea lentils in English) together with onions, tomatoes, herbs and spices. Relish our favorite dal recipe that is flavorful, mildly-spiced and creamy with roti, naan or your favorite steamed rice.


Prep Time Cook Time Total Time Servings
10 mins 15 mins 25 mins 4

Ingredients you will need

  • An onion and a tomato (both finely chopped for this dal recipe)
  • Green Chilies (according to your spice tolerance)
  • Curry Leaves
  • Asafoetida Powder (hing)
  • Coriander leaves (cilantro)
  • Kasuri Methi (dry fenugreek leaves)
  • Additional Seasonings (ginger, black mustard seeds, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, red chili powder, Garam Masala powder, oil, lemon juice, and salt)

Follow these steps to make this YUM!!

  1. Cook Dal
    1. Gather all ingredients.
    2. Rinse your lentils in water for 3 to 4 times. I would recommend using ½ cup tuvar dal (arhar dal or pigeon pea lentils) or ¼ cup each of tuvar dal and masoor dal (pink or orange lentils).
    3. After rinsing, pour the lentils into a 2-liter pressure cooker and toss in a pinch of turmeric powder and 1.5 cups of water.
    4. Pressure cook the lentils for 9 to 10 minutes on medium heat, or until they are thoroughly cooked and completely soft. Once lentils are cooked, mash them with a spoon or wired whisk, then set aside.
  2. Sauté Onions, Tomatoes, Spices
    1. Heat 2 to 3 tablespoons of oil, ghee or unsalted butter in a pan or skillet. Once hot, add ½ or ¾ teaspoon of black mustard seeds.
    2. When you hear the mustard seeds start crackling, add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and fry them with the mustard seeds for several seconds or until they change color. You should hear the same crackle for the cumin seeds!
    3. With the mustard and cumin seeds still in the pan, add ⅓ cup of chopped onions.
    4. Sauté the seeds and onion mixture until the onions become translucent or lightly browned.
    5. When the onions have lightly browned, add in 1 to 1.5 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste and stir to combine. Sauté the mixture for a few seconds or until the raw aroma of the ginger-garlic goes away on a low heat.
    6. Finally, add in 10 to 12 curry leaves, 2 to 3 dry red chilies, and 1 to 2 green chilies. Stir to combine with the ginger-garlic paste mixture.
    7. Mix well on a low heat, stirring to combine the flavors.
    8. Add all the spice powders and mix again!
      • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder (ground turmeric)
      • ½ teaspoon red chili powder or cayenne or smoked paprika (For a light smoky flavor)
      • 1 to 2 pinches of asafoetida, or hing (Skip if you do not have it.)
    9. After mixing thoroughly, add about ⅓ to ½ cup of finely chopped tomatoes.
    10. Stir together one more time and continue to sauté until the tomatoes become soft and pulpy. The oil should also start to leave the masala mixture, which is an indication that the onion-tomato base mixture is fully cooked.
  3. Make Dal Fry
    1. Add your mashed lentils to sautéed onion-tomato masala mixture in the pan or skillet.
    2. Combine and mix everything evenly with the rest of the masala mixture.
    3. Pour 1 to 1.5 cups of water. You can adjust the consistency of the dal by adding more or less water as per your taste preferences.
    4. Mix and stir thoroughly to get a smooth and even consistency.
    5. Season with salt as per taste. Stir and mix again.
    6. Simmer for 5 to 6 minutes on low to medium heat. Stir occasionally, so that the lentils do not stick to the bottom of the pan.
    7. Add 1 teaspoon of crushed kasoori methi (dried fenugreek leaves) and sprinkle ¼ to ½ teaspoon of garam masala powder. Simmer for another minute
  4. Garnish & Serve
    1. Turn off the heat and add 2 tablespoons of chopped coriander leaves. You can also add ½ to 1 teaspoon lemon juice at this step for a little added freshness and tang.
    2. Stir just one last time.
    3. Serve Dal Fry hot or warm with paratha, roti, jeera rice or plain steamed basmati rice accompanied with an optional Onion Raita, papad, lime/lemon slices or mango pickle or onion salad or the favorite Kachumber Salad.
